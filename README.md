# VPN Privacy and Secure Communication Report

## Objective
Demonstrate the use of a free VPN client to protect privacy and secure communication.

## VPN Service Used
ProtonVPN (Free Tier)

## VPN Setup Steps

1. **Sign Up and Account Creation**
   - Created a free ProtonVPN account at https://protonvpn.com.
   - Verified email and logged into the account.

2. **Download and Installation**
   - Downloaded ProtonVPN client for my operating system.
   - Installed the client following on-screen instructions.

3. **Connecting to VPN Server**
   - Opened ProtonVPN and logged in.
   - Selected a free server (closest location recommended).
   - Clicked "Connect" and waited for connection confirmation.

4. **Verify IP Address Change**
   - Visited (https://whatismyipaddress.com) to check the public IP.
   - Confirmed the IP address had changed to the VPN server location.

5. **Testing Encrypted Traffic**
   - Browsed to various sites to ensure secure and encrypted traffic.
   - Verified connection status as "Connected" in ProtonVPN.

6. **Disconnect and Speed Comparison**
   - Disconnected VPN and rechecked IP address on whatismyipaddress.com.
   - Noted browsing speed differences with and without VPN enabled.

## VPN Encryption and Privacy Features
- ProtonVPN uses AES-256 encryption and secure protocols like OpenVPN and WireGuard.
- No-logs policy ensures user activity is not recorded or stored.
- VPN masks real IP addresses, preventing tracking and enhancing privacy.
- Encrypts data traffic protecting it from ISPs, attackers, or unsecured WiFi threats.

## VPN Benefits
- Provides privacy by hiding your real IP and location.
- Secures internet traffic via encryption, protecting sensitive data.
- Enables bypassing geo-restrictions and censorship.
- Helps protect user identity and online activity from third parties.

## VPN Limitations
- Free-tier servers may have limited speed and server selections.
- VPNs cannot prevent all forms of tracking such as browser fingerprinting.
- Some websites may block known VPN IPs.
- VPN providers might log connection metadata, potentially impacting anonymity.
